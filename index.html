Projects

Classifying Skin Lesion Images

My project for the 2017 Big Data Summer Institute at the University of Michigan was to build a model that could classify photos of skin lesions as either benign or malignant. My project group extracted many image features and tried several different machine learning algorithms. We found AdaBoost (adaptive boosting) to be the most accurate, with a final average accuracy of 71.4 percent. Download the poster here.

Hoosier CrossFit Membership Analysis

This was the final project for STAT-S 432. Shaun Tieman, co-owner of Hoosier CrossFit, asked our class to analyze some membership data for him. My group chose to examine class attendance trends based on time of day, season, and weekend vs. weekday. Read the final report here.
