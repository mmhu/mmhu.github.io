Projects

Investigation of suPAR as a Biomarker for Renal Dysfunction

This was a group case study for the 2018 Summer Institute for Training in Biostatistics (SIBS) at Emory University to explore the relationship between the protein suPAR and renal dysfunction in patients with chronic kidney disease and/or its comorbidities. We also did Kaplan-Meier and Cox proportional hazards survival analysis for major adverse cardiovascular events. Read the report here.

I also presented this project with a fellow SIBS student at the StatFest 2018 poster session. Download the poster here.

Effect of Mycorrhizal Fungi on Leaf Litter Decomposition

Undergraduates in the Department of Statistics at IU take Statistical Consulting (STAT-S 490) as a capstone course in collaboration with the Indiana Statistical Consulting Center. I wrote reports for several researchers in different fields, including ecology. For this project, I examined the effect of the two main types of mycorrhizal fungi on leaf litter quality across biomes. Read the report here.

Classifying Skin Lesion Images

My project for the 2017 Big Data Summer Institute at the University of Michigan was to build a model that could classify photos of skin lesions as either benign or malignant. My project group extracted several image features and ran many different machine learning algorithms. We found AdaBoost (adaptive boosting) to be the most accurate, with a final average accuracy of 71.4 percent. Download the poster here.

Hoosier CrossFit Membership Analysis

This was the final project for Applied Linear Models II (STAT-S 432). Shaun Tieman, co-owner of Hoosier CrossFit, asked our class to analyze some gym membership data for him. My group chose to examine class attendance trends based on time of day, season, and weekend vs. weekday. Read the final report here.
