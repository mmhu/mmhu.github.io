Projects

Case Study: Biomarkers for Renal Dysunction

This was a group case study for the 2018 Summer Institute for Training in Biostatistics (SIBS) at Emory University to explore the relationship between the suPAR protein and renal dysfunction in patients with chronic kidney disease and/or its comorbidities. We wrote a report to be presented to non-statisticians (i.e. clinicians). Read the report here.

Classifying Skin Lesion Images

My project for the 2017 Big Data Summer Institute at the University of Michigan was to build a model that could classify photos of skin lesions as either benign or malignant. My project group extracted several image features and ran many different machine learning algorithms. We found AdaBoost (adaptive boosting) to be the most accurate, with a final average accuracy of 71.4 percent. Download the poster here.

Hoosier CrossFit Membership Analysis

This was the final project for STAT-S 432. Shaun Tieman, co-owner of Hoosier CrossFit, asked our class to analyze some gym membership data for him. My group chose to examine class attendance trends based on time of day, season, and weekend vs. weekday. Read the final report here.
